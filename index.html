What is happening?

Every person receives the message from someone else. When they repeat it, the message has a small chance of changing.

The simulation models three common effects:

  • Uncertainty loss: "might" can become "will."
  • Detail loss: specific details disappear.
  • Exaggeration: ordinary events can sound larger.

Scientific honesty

This demonstrates how repeated transmission can create distortion. It does not prove that real rumors always behave this way. Real people also use judgment, emotions, trust, memory, and social context.
